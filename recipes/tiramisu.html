Cheesecake Recipe

Description

This classic tiramisu is light, creamy, and layered with rich coffee flavor. Featuring soft espresso-soaked ladyfingers and a smooth mascarpone filling, it delivers the perfect balance of sweetness and bitterness. Made with simple ingredients and no baking required, this dessert is easy to prepare yet elegant enough to impress. Ideal for dinner parties, special occasions, or a luxurious homemade treat.

Ingredients

  • Coffee at room temperature
  • Espresso powder
  • Kahlua
  • Egg Yolks
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Mascarpone Cheese
  • Heavy Cream
  • Vanilla Extract
  • Ladyfingers (Savoiardi)
  • Dutch Processed Cocoa Powder

Directions

  1. Make the Coffee Soak: Brew the coffee and stir in the espresso powder and Kahlua. Cool completely.
  2. Whip Egg Yolks and Sugar: Beat the raw egg yolks and sugar until thick and pale. Add a little additional Kahlua and whisk until combined.
  3. Add Mascarpone: Add the mascarpone to the egg yolks and beat until no lumps remain.
  4. Whip the Cream: Beat the heavy cream to medium-stiff peaks. Fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture.
  5. Dip and Layer Ladyfingers: Quickly roll the ladyfingers through the coffee mixture until the cookies have absorbed some of the coffee but are not soggy. Arrange half the ladyfingers in a baking dish.
  6. Spread the Filling: Spread half the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers, dust with cocoa powder, and then repeat with another layer of the soaked ladyfingers and the remaining mascarpone.
  7. Chill: Refrigerate the tiramisu for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight. Just before serving, dust with additional cocoa powder or chocolate shavings.
